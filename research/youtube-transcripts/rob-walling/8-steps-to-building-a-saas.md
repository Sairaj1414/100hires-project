# 8 Steps to Building a SaaS

**Expert:** Rob Walling  
**Source:** YouTube  
**Topic:** SaaS Validation and Product Development Framework

## Summary

In this video, Rob Walling outlines a simplified 8-step framework for building a SaaS business from scratch. He emphasizes that while the framework is straightforward, success requires consistent execution and customer-focused thinking.

## The 8 Steps to Building SaaS

### 1. Identify Your Unfair Advantage (0:27)

- Build relationships with founders and industry peers.
- Strong networks often provide better opportunities than a large audience.
- Leverage existing expertise and connections.

### 2. Start With a Problem (3:43)

- Focus on solving real customer problems.
- Keep a record of pain points you observe.
- Avoid becoming attached to a solution before validating the problem.

### 3. Evaluate Potential Solutions (4:37)

- Software is not always the best answer.
- Productized services and manual processes can sometimes solve problems more effectively.
- Consider alternative approaches before building software.

### 4. Use an Evaluation Framework (5:46)

- Assess market demand, founder fit, and problem importance.
- Use structured frameworks to reduce emotional decision-making.
- Validate ideas before investing heavily.

### 5. Differentiate Your Offering (6:10)

- Avoid building a commodity product.
- Develop a unique positioning strategy.
- Clearly communicate why customers should choose your solution.

### 6. Talk to Customers (7:07)

- Conduct customer interviews.
- Validate assumptions with real users.
- Use unbiased questioning techniques.

### 7. Start Marketing Early (8:41)

- Create a landing page before building the product.
- Collect email addresses from interested prospects.
- Measure demand before investing in development.

### 8. Build the MVP (9:27)

- Launch the simplest version of the product.
- Use no-code tools or manual processes when appropriate.
- Focus on delivering value quickly.

## Bonus Step: Phased Launch (12:15)

- Release the product to small groups of users.
- Collect feedback and fix issues early.
- Reduce churn and improve onboarding.

## Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS

1. Start with customer problems, not product ideas.
2. Validate demand before building.
3. Build audience and marketing channels early.
4. Differentiate through positioning.
5. Launch small, learn fast, and iterate.

## Research Notes

This video is highly relevant because it provides a complete framework for SaaS idea validation, customer discovery, MVP development, and go-to-market planning. Many of these principles align directly with successful B2B SaaS content and growth strategies.
