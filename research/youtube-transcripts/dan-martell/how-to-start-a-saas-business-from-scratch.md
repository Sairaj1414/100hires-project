# How to Start a SaaS Business From Scratch

**Expert:** Dan Martell  
**Source:** YouTube  
**Date Collected:** 24/06/2026  
**Topic:** SaaS Validation, MVP Development, Product-Market Fit, Growth Strategy

## Summary

Dan Martell shares a practical seven-step framework for building a SaaS business using a lean startup approach. The video emphasizes validating customer problems before writing code, launching an MVP quickly, collecting continuous customer feedback, and using scalable acquisition channels to achieve sustainable growth.

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Start with the Problem (0:32)
- Identify painful problems instead of chasing startup ideas.
- Talk directly with customers.
- Look for industries with growing demand and operational challenges.

### 2. Build a Prototype (4:31)
- Create clickable wireframes before development.
- Test usability and user flow.
- Reduce engineering costs through early validation.

### 3. Validate Your Product (8:29)
- Secure paying customers before building.
- Launch an early adopter program.
- Confirm willingness to pay.

### 4. Build Your MVP (12:44)
- Limit development to roughly three months.
- Focus only on essential features.
- Use contractors or no-code tools where appropriate.

### 5. Collect Customer Feedback (16:01)
- Conduct customer interviews regularly.
- Improve retention through user feedback.
- Focus on active users rather than only the loudest opinions.

### 6. Generate Demand (20:31)
Growth channels include:
- Content marketing
- Paid advertising
- Partnerships
- Press

Partnerships can accelerate early customer acquisition by leveraging existing audiences.

### 7. Find a Growth Hack (24:32)
- Discover unique acquisition channels.
- Build competitive advantages.
- Look for creative ways to reach your ideal customers.

## Why this matters for B2B SaaS

- Demonstrates lean SaaS validation.
- Shows customer-first product development.
- Explains scalable acquisition strategies.
- Provides a structured framework that founders can immediately apply.
