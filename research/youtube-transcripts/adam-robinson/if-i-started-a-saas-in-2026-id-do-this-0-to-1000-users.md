# If I Started a SaaS in 2026, I'd Do This (0 to 1,000+ Users)

**Expert:** Adam Robinson  
**Source:** YouTube  
**Topic:** SaaS Growth, Customer Discovery, Product-Market Fit, Founder-Led Marketing

## Summary

In this video, Adam Robinson presents a step-by-step framework for launching a SaaS or AI company from zero to more than 1,000 paying users. Rather than building a product first, he recommends validating customer demand through direct conversations, achieving product-market fit, and then scaling through content, outreach, and retargeting.

## Key Themes

### Discovery Process (0:30 - 5:20)

- Conduct at least 50 customer discovery calls before building.
- Validate problems with real prospects.
- Deliver value manually in the early stages.
- Stay close to customers and iterate quickly.
- Avoid automating too early.

### Signals of Product-Market Fit (5:22 - 6:16)

A product has achieved product-market fit when:

- Customers refer new users consistently.
- Users would be disappointed if the product disappeared.
- Growth continues without heavy marketing.

### The Magic Triangle (7:12 - 14:14)

#### Content

- Build a founder brand on LinkedIn.
- Publish at least three valuable posts each week.
- Share your building journey, lessons, and industry insights.

#### Outreach

- Target your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).
- Follow up with website visitors.
- Personalize outreach rather than relying on mass email campaigns.

#### Retargeting

- Install tracking pixels on your website.
- Stay visible to interested prospects.
- Create ads that answer who, what, why, and how.

### Building in Public

- Share progress openly.
- Build trust through transparency.
- Focus on authenticity rather than polished marketing.

## Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS YouTube Strategy

1. Build an audience before aggressively selling.
2. Document your startup journey through educational content.
3. Founder-led content increases trust and credibility.
4. Validate customer demand before investing heavily in product development.
5. Combine content, outreach, and retargeting into a repeatable growth system.

## Research Notes
