# Student to AI Agency to $9,000/Month AI Real Estate SaaS in 6 Months

**Expert:** Liam Ottley  
**Guest:** Humza Ahmed  
**Source:** YouTube  
**Topic:** AI SaaS, Validation, Niche Selection, Customer Acquisition, Founder Journey

## Summary

In this interview, Liam Ottley speaks with Humza Ahmed, an entrepreneur who went from being a university student to building a $9,000/month AI SaaS business within six months. The discussion covers how he identified a niche market, validated demand, transitioned from an AI agency to a SaaS business, and acquired his first customers.

## Key Themes

### The Catalyst (1:22 - 2:56)

- Humza automated a tedious internship task using AI.
- A process that previously took months was completed in minutes.
- This experience revealed the business potential of AI automation.
- He joined Liam Ottley's AI accelerator to start his entrepreneurial journey.

### Finding a Niche (3:05 - 5:12)

- Generic outreach produced weak results.
- He focused on the real estate appraisal industry.
- Success came from understanding industry-specific pain points.
- Specialized solutions created stronger demand than general services.

### Pivoting to SaaS (5:13 - 6:28)

- Initial services evolved into a dedicated software platform.
- The product became Automax, serving property appraisers.
- SaaS offered recurring revenue and greater scalability.
- Customer demand drove product development decisions.

### Strategic Growth (8:28 - 9:07)

- Discussions began with a major appraisal company.
- Enterprise opportunities emerged after solving a clear problem.
- Industry partnerships accelerated growth potential.

### Advice for Beginners (15:23 - 23:28)

#### Validate First

- Confirm demand before building.
- Seek customer commitments early.
- Use Letters of Intent (LOIs) where possible.

#### Prioritize Warm Outreach

- Leverage existing relationships.
- Use personal networks for initial introductions.
- Early trust accelerates customer acquisition.

#### Personalize Outreach

- Avoid generic mass messaging.
- Research prospects before contacting them.
- Reference real business challenges and public activity.
